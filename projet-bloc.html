what AIESEC gives to human kind :

AIESEC is a non-political, independent, not-for-profit organization run by students and recent graduates of institutions of higher education. Our members are interested in world issues, leadership, and management. AIESEC does not discriminate on the basis of ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, religion or national/social origin.

More about us:

We are passionately driven by one cause, peace and fulfillment of humankind's potential. AIESEC and the United Nations have a rich relationship of mutual belief and passion towards achieving peace in the world. We firmly believes that young people need to be at the center of the global development process. And so AIESEC and the United Nations have been working together to ensure that the world that we create tomorrow is aligned with the needs of the youth today.

How AIESEC is promoting cultural exchanges :

AIESEC is a global platform for young people to develop their leadership potential through international internships and volunteer opportunities. Founded in 1948, AIESEC is a non-governmental, and not-for-profit organization entirely run by youth for youth.Our Global Talent program connects companies with young bright minds and help them fulfill their business needs with international students and recent graduates.

what AIESEC has been giving to its members :

AIESEC is more than an association, it is an experience. As a matter of fact, AIESECers get to know each other as much as they get to know themselves by living, sharing, learning with another:
  • Induction Day: this day is the induction process for the new members to introduce them to the AIESEC experience. First impressions are vital to establishing strong relationships between members.
    Anyway, time for our newies to share with you!
    1. Why did you choose to join AIESEC ?

      “When I received the email proposing to apply, I hesitated a lot. I was clearly afraid of being alone in a world I didn't know, but on the one hand, this fear was my new challenge. I also wanted to do something, by which I mean: "to be part of something" and to be able to have new life goals that are more stimulating in the short term than just succeeding in my studies.” Chiara di Bernardo

    2. How did you experience Induction day ?

      “I immediately felt that this day was fully up to my expectations, I especially perceived that it was much more than an association but really a big family with lots of nice and motivated people who make us want to give the best of ourselves in this adventure.” Lorenzo Bernasconi

  • Kick-off: in this event, time to deal with the real purpose of AIESEC! Swiss Local committees gather themselves to talk about the importance of developing leadership skills and improving the SDG's awareness.

    "Kick-off is an amazing experience, which allowed us all to get closer! What I also love is the fact that either as a member, a VP, or an LCP, we are still learning! Here is one of the AIESEC strengths, everything is focused on humans and knowledge."Olga Bastiat, Vice-President of Innovation.

    "Kick-Off 2021 was a really challenging experience for me since it was my first AIESEC national conference as president of LC Lausanne. Fortunately, everything went well and we spent 3 very interesting days where we all learned a lot about AIESEC." Mélanie Demaurex, Local Comitee President.

    "As soon as I joined AIESEC, one of the first things I heard was: "conferences are the best things you can attend to in AIESEC". Although Kick-off was online, I had the opportunity to get closer with many people and to learn lots of new skills just like time management or team spirit." Maher Boukris, Vice-President of Business Development.

  • One of Our Alumni also wants to share with you :

    "My experience in AIESEC in Lausanne represents an important part of my student life. Indeed, beyond the people I met and the things I was able to learn as a member, My Vice-President position took my AIESEC experience a big step further. Becoming a VP allowed me to work with MY team, take real initiatives and finally learn to assume my entire role as a member of the executive committee." Mohamed Ayadi, Ex-Vice-President Finance/Legalities.

what YOU could give to AIESEC :

AIESEC in Lausanne is recruting!

A new semester is starting for all of us, but also for AIESEC who is recruiting its newbies every beginning of the semester. By joining our association, you will have the opportunity to give a dynamic to your university years, to discover the professional world, and to share values related to multiculturalism. The resumption of classroom teaching with protective measures will also allow you to visit our office on campus, to meet our members, and to surpass yourself by working as a team in a global crisis.

You can apply to one of the following teams:
  • Business Development
  • Marketing et Communication
  • Outgoing Exchanges
  • Incoming Exchanges
  • Finance and Legalities
  • Talent Management
  • Innovation
